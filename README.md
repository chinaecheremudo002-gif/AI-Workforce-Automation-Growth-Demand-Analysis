# Introduction
Artificial Intelligence (AI) has become one of the most transformative forces shaping modern industries and economies. Across different countries and sectors, organizations are increasingly adopting AI to improve productivity, reduce operational costs, and accelerate decision-making processes. From automation and predictive analytics to intelligent systems, AI is changing how businesses operate and compete in the global economy.
However, the rapid adoption of AI also brings significant workforce challenges. While AI creates new opportunities and emerging job roles, it can also lead to job displacement and automation risks, especially in tasks that are repetitive or rule-based. In some industries and countries, the number of jobs created through AI-driven innovation may exceed the number of jobs at risk, while in others the opposite may occur, making certain roles increasingly redundant.
This project explores the relationship between AI adoption, automation risk, investment, productivity growth, cost reduction, and workforce demand across different countries, regions, and job sectors. The goal is to provide data-driven insights into how AI is reshaping the future of work and to identify where economies are gaining opportunities or facing disruption.

# Problem Statement

The rapid adoption of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is reshaping the global workforce, creating both opportunities and uncertainty for organizations, policymakers, and professionals. While AI improves efficiency, productivity, and innovation, it also raises important concerns such as job displacement, skill mismatch, and the risk of certain roles becoming obsolete. In addition, the benefits of AI are not evenly distributed across countries, industries, and job sectors, leading to unequal workforce impacts. This makes it increasingly important to analyze where AI is driving job creation and economic growth, and where it is contributing to workforce disruption.
